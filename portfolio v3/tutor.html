Project Description:

Tutor Time is an application I designed that helps connect high school students with qualified and knowledgable tutors. The application is designed to work similarly to a text messaging service, where students can work collaboratively on their homework or other academic goals with a tutor. By focusing on a smart phone application, Tutor Time is a new way to help students learn without having to work through in-person tutoring sessions.

Problem & Target Audience

Tutor Time was aimed at solving one of the largest problems facing teenagers today; their academics. According to America's Promised Alliance, "The U.S. has a 30 percent rate of students failing to graduate high school." Additionally, the traditional U.S. high school experience has stayed fundamentally the same for decades, which provided an opportunity for a new solution to be developed. Given these issues, I wanted to concentrate on finding a way for high school students to receive the help they need, while taking advantage of newer technologies that exist, such as the smart phone.

User Interviews & Personas

The most important part of this project was conducting user interviews with current and former high school students to learn more about their academic endeavors. Specifically, I wanted to learn more about times when they were struggling to complete a homework assignment or failed to grasp new materials they were learning. Additionally, I wanted to gain a better understanding about which tools and resources they had used, and gauge how effective they were at helping students out. After conducting user interviews, I was able to create 3 different personas that helped serve as a guide for the rest of the design process.

Usability Testing

After paper prototypes were created, I moved onto the next step in the design process, usability testing. I needed to test them out with members of my target audience to gather valuable feedback and insights about what works, what doesn't, and what I can do to improve the experience. I found several participants to go through the testing sessions with; each participant went through 10 small tasks that could be accomplished by my application.

Key Insights from Usability Testing:

The usability testing sessions revealed a lot of helpful information regarding my current designs. For the most part, participants were able to accomplish the tasks I asked of them, but there were several instances where the experience became either confusing or frustrating. I used these instances to gather as much feedback as possible, and actively sought out advice from my participants on how to fix any issues they came across. With their help, I was able to further refine my paper prototypes, and move on to the next stage of the design process, creating higher fidelity mockups.

Medium and High Fidelity Mockups
